About Us

Namba Coffee Estate is a premier Kenyan coffee estate located in the lush highlands of Kenya. Founded as a family-owned farm, our estate has grown into a hub for smallholder farmers in the region. Our vision is to empower smallholders while producing world-class coffee through sustainable practices, quality focus, and community development.

Over the years we have worked tirelessly to build a reputation for excellence. We partner with farmers, sharing knowledge on cultivation and processing, and we strive to protect our environment through responsible farming. Our mission is to deliver exceptional Kenyan coffee to the world while ensuring fair income for those who grow it.

Certifications: We pursue certifications such as Rainforest Alliance and Fairtrade to demonstrate our commitment to sustainable and ethical coffee.

Our Coffee

We cultivate classic Kenyan Arabica varieties including SL28, SL34, Ruiru 11, and Batian. Grown at high altitudes in rich volcanic soils, these beans develop bright acidity with complex berry and citrus notes. Our estate’s microclimate and careful husbandry bring out the best in each variety.

  • Varieties: SL28, SL34, Ruiru 11, Batian
  • Altitude: 1,600–1,800 m
  • Processing: Washed, natural, and honey methods
  • Harvest calendar: Main crop October–December; fly crop May–June

Farmer Network

Namba Coffee Estate is more than a farm—we serve as a cooperative hub for hundreds of smallholder farmers. We provide training on agronomy, harvesting, and processing, and we guarantee traceability and fair pricing for their cherries. Through our partnership model, farmers deliver ripe cherries to our washing station where they are weighed, pulped, and processed with care.

We believe in transparency and community, and we proudly share stories from our farmers. Here are a few testimonials:

  • Jane Mwangi: “With Namba Coffee Estate I learned better pruning techniques and now my yields have doubled.”
  • Peter Njoroge: “I receive fair payments on time and know exactly where my coffee ends up.”

Processing & Quality Control

Quality begins at the washing station. Cherries are sorted, pulped and fermented in clean tanks, then washed and soaked in fresh water. We dry our coffee on raised beds under the sun, constantly turning and covering it at night to ensure even drying. Once dried to the right moisture content, the coffee is hulled, graded, and stored under controlled conditions until export.

Our quality control team monitors each stage of processing. We cup every lot and only the finest coffees are selected for auction or direct export. Our dedication to excellence ensures that every cup delivers the distinctive Kenyan profile that buyers love.

Sales & Auction

At Namba Coffee Estate we grade and prepare lots according to the Kenya Coffee Board standards. Most of our coffee is sold through the Nairobi Coffee Exchange, where buyers bid for lots based on cup quality and traceability. We also invite roasters and importers to request samples and purchase directly. Contact us to learn more about our current offerings and auction results.

Sustainability & Impact

Our commitment goes beyond quality—we strive to protect the environment and uplift our community. We implement water recycling, compost organic waste, and shade our coffee trees with indigenous species. We support gender inclusion and youth training, and we reinvest in local schools and healthcare. By empowering farmers and preserving nature, we ensure that Kenyan coffee can thrive for generations.
